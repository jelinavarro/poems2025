11:59 PM
Written by Jeli Navarro

Starting my homework at 11:59
is like moving to the city alone.

At 11:59, the wind sneaks
behind the room's white door
as the quillow blocks the way.
The chair creeks without a care.
Through the window starlight shines,
touching the edge of the keyboard.

Notifications blind the eyes like the
shimmering stars of the night,
ensuring both eyes are aware——
it only has a second.
For as soon as the eyes remember,
a photo is taken.
A screenshoot that is as slow as
buying a snack from a vending
machine.

Tick tock, I hear the clock.
I cannot bide my time.
